2. What actions are possible?

Identify the interactions you can take on the page. This will include buttons, text fields, and so on.

Don't forget about actions that change the perceived state without changing data. This includes actions such as expanding and collapsing items, filtering, and sorting.

Accessibility areas affected:

  • buttons vs. links
  • text field types (text, number, email, etc.)
  • keyboard navigation
  • choosing components with the most appropriate semantics
